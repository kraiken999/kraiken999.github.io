Introduction

Plastic waste has reached unprecedented levels in human history. Today, we explore plastoline, a renewable fuel created through microwave-assisted pyrolysis (MAP), and how this technology can help clean both our planet and our bodies.

“Plastic never goes away — it just breaks down into finer and finer particles.” — Desiree LaBeaud, MD (Stanford Medicine)

Since the 1950s, humanity has produced 6.3 billion metric tons of plastic waste. That equals roughly 7.2 trillion grocery bags—enough to reach the moon and back 5,790 times.

What is Plastoline?

Plastoline is a fuel created by converting plastic waste into usable hydrocarbons through MAP. It matters because it transforms a global pollution crisis into a renewable energy source powered by solar energy.

How MAP (Microwave-Assisted Pyrolysis) Works

MAP uses microwaves and high heat in a low-oxygen chamber to break plastic down into crude-oil-like fuel.

Process Overview

  • Plastic is inserted into a sealed pyrolysis chamber.
  • The chamber is heated to 450°C.
  • Microwaves accelerate molecular breakdown.
  • Plastic decomposes into gas and melted hydrocarbons.
  • Gas is condensed into liquid fuel (plastoline).

Microplastics and Human Health

Microplastics and nanoplastics are now found in the brain, heart, testicles, placenta, lymph nodes, stomach, urine, semen, breastmilk, and newborn meconium.

Humans ingest or inhale an estimated 74,000–121,000 particles per year.

Fertility Effects

Additives like phthalates and BPA disrupt sperm development and epigenetic programming, affecting future generations.

Julian Brown: The Inventor Behind MAP

Julian Brown began building MAP machines at age 17. His work demonstrates that innovation can begin in a backyard and scale into global sustainability efforts.

Social and Cultural Change

A major question arises: Does plastoline require us to keep producing plastic?

Short-term: We already have more than enough plastic waste to sustain MAP for decades.

Long-term: Policy and culture must prevent industries from producing excess plastic simply to maintain fuel supply.

Ideal Three-Phase Future

  1. Clean up existing global plastic waste using MAP.
  2. Transition plastoline into a bridge fuel.
  3. Shift to other renewable sources (thermal, wave, kinetic sidewalks, etc.).
